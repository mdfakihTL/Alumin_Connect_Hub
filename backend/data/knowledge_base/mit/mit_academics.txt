MIT ACADEMICS GUIDE

ACADEMIC STRUCTURE

Schools at MIT:
1. School of Architecture and Planning
2. School of Engineering
3. School of Humanities, Arts, and Social Sciences
4. Sloan School of Management
5. School of Science
6. Schwarzman College of Computing

UNDERGRADUATE PROGRAMS

Degrees Offered:
- Bachelor of Science (S.B.)
- 50+ undergraduate majors
- Option to pursue double majors or minors

Popular Majors:
- Computer Science and Engineering (Course 6)
- Mechanical Engineering (Course 2)
- Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (Course 6-2)
- Mathematics (Course 18)
- Physics (Course 8)
- Biology (Course 7)
- Economics (Course 14)
- Brain and Cognitive Sciences (Course 9)

General Institute Requirements (GIRs):
- Science Core: Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Calculus
- Humanities, Arts, and Social Sciences (HASS): 8 subjects
- Restricted Electives in Science and Technology (REST)
- Laboratory requirement
- Communication requirement
- Physical education requirement

GRADUATE PROGRAMS

MIT offers over 60 graduate programs including:
- Master of Science (SM)
- Master of Engineering (MEng)
- Doctor of Philosophy (PhD)
- Master of Business Administration (MBA) - Sloan
- Various professional master's degrees

ACADEMIC CALENDAR
- Fall semester: September - December
- Independent Activities Period (IAP): January
- Spring semester: February - May

GRADING SYSTEM
- Letter grades: A, B, C, D, F
- First semester freshman year: Pass/No Record
- Pass/Fail options available for some electives

RESEARCH OPPORTUNITIES

Undergraduate Research Opportunities Program (UROP):
- 85% of undergraduates participate
- Paid research positions available
- Work with MIT faculty and researchers
- Can count toward academic credit

Major Research Centers:
- MIT Lincoln Laboratory
- MIT Media Lab
- Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL)
- Koch Institute for Integrative Cancer Research
- Energy Initiative (MITEI)
- Institute for Data, Systems, and Society (IDSS)

ACADEMIC SUPPORT
- Office of Academic Support
- Tutoring services
- Writing and Communication Center
- Academic advisors in each department

CLASS SIZES
- Average class size: 27 students
- Student-faculty ratio: 3:1
- 71% of classes have fewer than 20 students
