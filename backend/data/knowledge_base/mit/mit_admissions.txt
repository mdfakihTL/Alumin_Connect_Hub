MIT ADMISSIONS GUIDE - Massachusetts Institute of Technology

ABOUT MIT ADMISSIONS
MIT is one of the world's leading research universities, located in Cambridge, Massachusetts. The institute admits approximately 1,500 first-year students each year from a pool of over 25,000 applicants.

UNDERGRADUATE ADMISSION REQUIREMENTS

1. Academic Requirements:
- High School Diploma or equivalent
- Recommended courses: 4 years of English, Mathematics (through calculus), 2+ years of history/social studies, Biology, Chemistry, Physics
- SAT or ACT scores (Note: Test-flexible policy may apply)
- TOEFL or IELTS for international students

2. Application Components:
- Common Application or Coalition Application
- MIT-specific essays
- Two teacher recommendations (one math/science, one humanities)
- One counselor recommendation
- Application fee ($75) or fee waiver
- Interview (optional but recommended)

3. Important Deadlines:
- Early Action: November 1 (decision by mid-December)
- Regular Action: January 1 (decision by mid-March)

SELECTION CRITERIA
MIT uses a holistic admissions process evaluating:
- Academic excellence and intellectual curiosity
- Collaborative spirit and teamwork
- Initiative and risk-taking
- Hands-on creativity
- Character and alignment with MIT's mission

FINANCIAL AID
MIT meets 100% of demonstrated financial need for all admitted students. Over 60% of undergraduates receive MIT scholarships. Average scholarship covers approximately $55,000 per year.
