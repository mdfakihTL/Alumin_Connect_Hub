MIT CAMPUS LIFE GUIDE

LIVING AT MIT
MIT offers a vibrant campus life with numerous housing options, activities, and support services for students.

RESIDENTIAL LIFE

Undergraduate Housing:
- 12 undergraduate residence halls
- All first-year students live on campus
- Housing guaranteed for 4 years
- Options include traditional dorms, suites, and independent living groups

Graduate Housing:
- 6 graduate residence halls
- Housing available but not guaranteed
- Options in Cambridge and Boston areas

DINING OPTIONS
MIT Dining operates multiple locations across campus:
- Maseeh Hall Dining (residential dining)
- Next House Dining
- Simmons Hall Dining
- McCormick Hall Dining
- Various cafes and food courts
- Meal plans available for all students

STUDENT ACTIVITIES

Athletics:
- MIT Engineers - NCAA Division III
- 31 varsity sports teams
- Club and intramural sports
- Zesiger Sports and Fitness Center

Student Organizations:
- Over 500 student organizations
- Academic and professional clubs
- Cultural and identity groups
- Performing arts groups
- Community service organizations
- Fraternities and sororities

Traditions:
- Campus Preview Weekend (CPW)
- Independent Activities Period (IAP) in January
- Hacking culture and creative pranks
- Boston Marathon Monday (Patriots' Day holiday)

HEALTH AND WELLNESS

MIT Medical:
- On-campus health services
- Mental health support
- 24/7 urgent care
- Insurance options available

Support Services:
- Student Support Services (S3)
- Mental health counseling
- Academic support
- Dean on Call program

CAMPUS FACILITIES
- Infinite Corridor (main campus hallway)
- MIT Libraries (5 libraries)
- Stata Center
- Media Lab
- MIT Museum
- Chapel and Religious Life

TRANSPORTATION
- Free MIT shuttles
- MBTA subway access (Kendall/MIT station)
- Charles River bike path
- Zipcar and bike sharing available
